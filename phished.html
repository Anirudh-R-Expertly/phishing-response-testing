What Just Happened?

You received what appeared to be a WhatsApp verification request. When you clicked the link, you were directed to a page that looked like an official WhatsApp service. In a real attack, this could have led to account theft or malware installation.

Red Flags You May Have Missed:
  • The message created a false sense of urgency about your account
  • The URL didn't match official WhatsApp domains (whatsapp.com)
  • The request was unexpected and unsolicited
  • The page asked for verification without proper context

Real WhatsApp Scams Targeting Your Contacts

Scenario 1: The "Hey Mom/Dad, I've Lost My Phone" Scam

A scammer impersonates your child or family member, claiming they've lost their phone and are using a temporary number. They then ask for money for an "emergency."

Hey Mom, this is my temporary number. I lost my phone and need to buy a new one. Can you send me $300? I'll pay you back tomorrow.
Scenario 2: The Verification Code Scam

A scammer pretends to be your friend who accidentally sent their WhatsApp verification code to you, asking you to forward it. In reality, they're trying to access your account.

Hi! Sorry, I accidentally sent my 6-digit code to your number. Can you forward it to me? It's urgent!
Scenario 3: The WhatsApp Gold/Plus Upgrade Scam

You receive a message about an exclusive "premium" version of WhatsApp with special features. The link installs malware.

WhatsApp Gold is finally available! 🎉 This exclusive version has video calling, 100+ emojis, enhanced security and more. Upgrade now: [malicious link]

How to Protect Yourself and Your Contacts

When Using WhatsApp:

  • Never share verification codes - WhatsApp will never ask for your 6-digit code, and neither will your contacts
  • Verify unusual requests - If a friend or family member makes an unusual request, call them on their known number to verify
  • Enable Two-Step Verification - Go to WhatsApp Settings > Account > Two-step verification
  • Check for the official verification - Official WhatsApp business accounts have a green checkmark
  • Be wary of "forwarded" labels - Messages forwarded many times may contain scams

How to Set Up WhatsApp Two-Step Verification

  1. Open WhatsApp and tap Settings (gear icon)
  2. Tap Account > Two-step verification > Enable
  3. Create a 6-digit PIN you'll remember
  4. Add your email address as a backup (optional but recommended)
  5. Tap Done

General Digital Safety Tips:

Remember: The real WhatsApp will never:
  • Ask for your password or verification code
  • Request payment information via message
  • Send unexpected verification links
  • Claim you need to "verify" or "authenticate" your account through a link
  • Offer special "premium" versions of the app
